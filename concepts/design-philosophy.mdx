## Mental Models

Before diving into ACE-Step 1.5, understanding the correct mental model is crucial for setting proper expectations and getting the most from the system.

### Human-Centered Generation

ACE-Step 1.5 is designed for **human-centered generation**, not **one-click generation**.

Understanding this distinction fundamentally changes how you approach AI music creation.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="One-Click Generation" icon="magic-wand-sparkles">
    **Client-Vendor Relationship**
    
    - Input prompt, click generate, pick result
    - AI is a service delivering to spec
    - Clear purpose, defined expectations
    - Same input → similar output
    - Limited ownership and control
    - Finite game: ends at delivery
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Human-Centered Generation" icon="hands-holding">
    **Creative Collaboration**
    
    - Iterative exploration and refinement
    - AI is an inspirer and co-creator
    - Playful discovery process
    - Same input → diverse possibilities
    - Full ownership and customization
    - Infinite game: process never ends
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### The Collaborative Workflow

Human-centered generation follows an iterative creative cycle:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Seed Inspiration">
    Throw out some initial creative seeds - a mood, style, or rough idea
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Explore Results">
    Generate several variations and listen for interesting directions
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Iterate & Refine">
    Choose promising directions and continue developing:
    - Adjust prompts to regenerate
    - Use **Cover** to maintain structure while changing details
    - Use **Repaint** for local modifications
    - Use **Add Layer** to build arrangements progressively
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Discover & Polish">
    Repeat until you discover something that resonates, then polish to completion
  </Step>
</Steps>

At this stage, AI is not a servant executing commands, but an **inspirer** helping you discover creative possibilities you might not have imagined.

## The Elephant Rider Metaphor

AI music generation is like the famous **elephant rider metaphor** from psychology.

### You Ride the Elephant

Consciousness rides on the subconscious, humans ride on elephants. You can give directions, but you can't make the elephant precisely and instantly execute every command. It has its own:

- **Inertia** - trained patterns and tendencies
- **Temperament** - preferences shaped by training data
- **Will** - inherent creative directions

This elephant is the music generation model.

### The Iceberg Model

Between audio and semantics lies a hidden iceberg:

<Note>
  What we can describe with language - style, instruments, timbre, emotion, scenes, progression, lyrics, vocal style - these are the **tiny tip above water**.
  
  The vast majority of what defines audio remains **beneath the surface**, inaccessible to text description.
</Note>

The most precise control would be: input the expected audio, receive it back unchanged.

But as long as you're using text descriptions, references, or prompts - **the model will have room to interpret**. This isn't a bug, it's the fundamental nature of the medium.

### What Shapes the Elephant?

The model is a fusion of countless elements:

- Data distribution
- Model scale and architecture  
- Algorithm design
- Annotation bias
- Evaluation bias

It's an **abstract crystallization of human music history and engineering trade-offs**.

### The Taste Problem

Even if we achieve technical "perfection," there's a fundamental challenge we can't avoid: **taste**.

Taste varies from person to person. If a music generation model tries to please all listeners, its output will tend toward the popular average of human music history - **this will be extremely mediocre**.

<Warning>
  When you find the model's output "not to your taste," this might not be the model's problem - **your taste may lie outside the average**. This is actually a good thing.
  
  It means: **You need to learn to guide this elephant, not expect it to automatically understand you.**
</Warning>

## Core Design Principles

For human-centered generation to truly work, several key conditions must be met:

### 1. Ownership Through Open Source

**The model must be open-source, locally runnable, and trainable.**

This isn't technical purism - it's about **fundamental creative ownership**.

<Accordion title="Why Local Ownership Matters">
  When you use closed-source platforms:
  - You don't own the model
  - Your works are bound by platform agreements
  - Version updates happen without your control
  - Terms can change unilaterally
  - Service can shut down anytime
  - No fine-tuning or customization possible
  
  When the model is open-source and locally runnable:
  - **You forever own this model**
  - **You forever own all creations made with it**
  - No third-party agreement hassles
  - No platform risks
  - Fine-tune and modify freely
  - Build your own creative system
  
  It's like buying an instrument - **you can use it anytime, anywhere, and adjust it however you want**.
</Accordion>

### 2. Speed Preserves Flow State

**The model must be fast.**

Human time is precious, but more importantly - **slow generation breaks flow state**.

<Card title="The Flow State Problem" icon="water">
  The core of human-centered workflow is the rapid cycle of "try, listen, adjust."
  
  If each generation takes minutes:
  - Your inspiration dissipates while waiting
  - The "play" experience degrades into a "wait" ordeal
  - Creative momentum is lost
  - Exploration becomes tedious
  
  **ACE-Step 1.5 is specifically optimized for this**: While ensuring quality, generation is fast enough to support a smooth human-machine dialogue rhythm.
</Card>

**Performance Targets:**

- **A100**: Under 2 seconds per full song
- **RTX 3090**: Under 10 seconds per full song  
- **Range**: 0.5-10 seconds depending on mode and steps

This speed enables true creative iteration without breaking concentration.

### 3. Finite Game vs Infinite Game

One-click generation is a **finite game** - clear goals, result-oriented, ends at the finish line. To some extent, it coldly industrializes music creation, replacing human roles.

Human-centered generation is an **infinite game** - because the fun lies in the process, and **the process never ends**.

<Note>
  Our vision is to **democratize AI music generation**. Let ACE-Step become a creative instrument in your pocket, let music return to **Play** itself - the creative "play," not just clicking play.
</Note>

## Accepting Uncertainty

### Randomness as a Feature

Randomness is a double-edged sword in generative models:

**Benefits:**
- **Explore creative space** - same input produces different variants
- **Discover unexpected surprises** - randomness brings results you didn't expect
- **Avoid repetition** - each generation is unique

**Challenges:**
- **Uncontrollable results** - can't precisely predict outcomes
- **Hard to reproduce** - difficult to recreate a specific good result
- **Tuning difficulty** - hard to isolate parameter effects from random variation
- **Screening cost** - need multiple generations to find satisfactory ones

### The Right Mindset

<Steps>
  <Step title="Accept Uncertainty">
    Randomness is an essential characteristic of AI music generation - not a bug, but a feature. Don't expect every generation to be perfect.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Embrace Exploration">
    Treat generation as "treasure hunting" - try multiple times, discover surprises. Enjoy the unexpected rather than obsessing over one-time success.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Fixed Seeds Wisely">
    - Want to understand parameter effects? **Fix seed** to eliminate randomness
    - Want to explore creative space? **Let seed vary** randomly
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Batch Generation + Smart Screening">
    Don't rely on single generations - batch generate and use automatic scoring for efficient screening.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Large Batch + AutoGen Solution

Because inference is extremely fast, you can explore random space efficiently:

<Accordion title="Batch Generation Strategy">
  **Set larger batch_size** (2, 4, 8) to generate multiple versions simultaneously
  
  **Enable AutoGen** to continuously generate new batches in background while you review:
  - Automatically uses same parameters with new random seeds
  - Ensures result diversity
  - Lets you "generate while listening"
  - Next batch ready when you finish reviewing current results
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Automatic Scoring">
  **DiT Lyrics Alignment Score** - evaluates alignment between lyrics and audio:
  - Higher score = more accurate lyric positioning
  - Better singing-lyrics match
  - Particularly valuable for lyrical music
  
  **Other Quality Metrics** - assess generation from multiple dimensions
  
  Use scores for initial screening, then manually select the best version for your needs.
</Accordion>

## Practical Philosophy

### Start Simple, Iterate Complex

Don't aim for perfection on the first try:

1. **Start with a rough direction** - simple prompt, basic idea
2. **Generate variations** - explore what the model produces
3. **Find interesting threads** - identify promising directions
4. **Refine progressively** - use Cover, Repaint, Add Layer to develop
5. **Polish to completion** - final touches when you've found the right path

### Learn to Guide, Not Command

Think of prompting as **suggesting direction** rather than issuing precise instructions:

- The model interprets your intent through its training
- Prompts anchor tendencies, they don't dictate exact output
- Work with the model's capabilities, not against them
- Discover what it does well and lean into those strengths

### Embrace the Collaborative Process

<Card title="You Are Co-Creators" icon="people-group">
  The best results come from **collaboration between human creativity and AI capability**:
  
  - You provide vision, taste, judgment, and curation
  - AI provides execution, variation, and inspiration
  - Together you explore creative spaces neither could access alone
  
  This is not automation replacing creativity - **it's augmentation expanding creative possibility**.
</Card>

## Why Open Source Matters

Beyond technical benefits, open source aligns with human-centered values:

### Transparency

You can inspect how the model works, understand its biases and limitations, and make informed creative decisions.

### Longevity  

Your creative tools and workflows won't disappear when a company changes direction or shuts down.

### Community

Collective improvement through shared knowledge, custom models, training techniques, and creative workflows.

### Evolution

Fine-tune models to your aesthetic, train LoRAs on specific styles, build specialized variants - **shape the tool to fit your creative vision**.

- **Own** your tools and creations

The goal isn't to press a button and receive a product.

The goal is to **engage in an infinite creative process** where AI becomes a genuine collaborator in your musical journey.
